# Google Acquires Fitbit (2021)

## The Deal
- **Acquirer:** Alphabet (Google)
- **Target:** Fitbit
- **Price:** $2.1 billion (cash)
- **Premium:** 71% over closing price at announcement
- **Date:** Announced Nov 2019, closed Jan 2021 (14-month regulatory review)

## Pre-Merger Financials

### Google/Alphabet (FY 2020)
- Revenue: $182.5B
- EBITDA: ~$54.6B (30% margin)
- Hardware revenue (Pixel, Nest, etc.): ~$6.8B (estimated)
- Wearables market share: 0% (no product)
- Health/fitness data: limited to Google Fit app (low adoption)

### Fitbit (FY 2020)
- Revenue: $1.43B (declining from $1.51B in 2019)
- EBITDA: -$191M (negative — losing money)
- Devices sold: 10M+ per year
- Active users: 29 million
- Health data: 12+ years of heart rate, sleep, activity data
- Market share: ~3% of wearables (behind Apple Watch at 28%, Samsung at 9%)

## Strategic Rationale
- Google had NO wearable device (Wear OS existed but no hardware)
- Fitbit had 29M active users with health data + brand recognition
- Health data + Google AI = personalized health insights
- Compete with Apple Watch ecosystem
- Regulatory risk: EU and DOJ scrutinized health data concerns

## Integration Type
**Related Diversification** — Google's AI/software + Fitbit's hardware/health data:
- Google's AI capabilities applied to Fitbit's health datasets
- Wear OS software + Fitbit hardware engineering
- Google's cloud infrastructure + Fitbit's user data pipeline
- Shared distribution through Google Store

## Post-Merger Results
- Fitbit brand maintained initially, then absorbed into Pixel Watch line
- Pixel Watch (2022) integrated Fitbit health tracking — reviewed well
- Fitbit app users pushed toward Google accounts (user backlash)
- Fitbit.com direct sales discontinued (consolidated to Google Store)
- EU required Google to keep Fitbit health data separate for 10 years
- Standalone Fitbit devices continued but received less investment
- Pixel Watch 2 and 3 deepened Fitbit integration
